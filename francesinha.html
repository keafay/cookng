Francesinha

Francesinha is a Portuguese sandwich originally from Porto, made with bread, wet-cured ham, linguiça, fresh sausage like chipolata, steak or roast meat, and covered with melted cheese and a hot thick tomato and beer sauce. It is typically served with french fries. Daniel da Silva, a returned emigrant from France and Belgium, tried to adapt the croque-madame (a ham and cheese sandwich topped with a fried egg) to the Portuguese taste when he moved to Porto. He first created the special sauce and populated the sandwich with local meats in 1953 at 'A Regaleira', a restaurant in Rua do Bonjardim, Porto;

Ingredients

Sandwich assembly

● 8 thick slices white bread

● 25g butter

● 200g thinly sliced ham

● 200g sirloin steak

● 200g chipotalas

● 200g chorizo

● 200g portuguese linguiça

● 12 slices cheddar cheese

● 4 eggs

● French fries

Francesinha Sauce

● 25g butter

● 300g chicken bones

● 1 small carrot, grated

● 1 small onion, grated

● 1/2 tsp salt

● 2 tbsp tomato paste

● 1 bay leaf

● 60ml port or whisky

● 375ml lager or light ale

● 1/2 cup milk

● 1 tbsp cornflour

● 2 tbsp cold water

● 1 tbsp tabasco sauce

How to make

1. For the Francesinha sauce, heat the butter in a medium-sized saucepan over high heat and add the beef trimmings or chicken bones and fry until well browned. Add the carrot, onion and salt and cook, stirring regularly, for five minutes until the vegetables are caramelised and fragrant. Stir through the tomato paste and cook a further minute, then add the bay leaf, port, beer and milk, and bring to a simmer. (If using chilli powder, add it at this stage.) Simmer for 30 minutes, mix the cornflour and water, stir it with the tabasco until the sauce is thickened. The sauce should be the consistency of thin gravy. Strain the sauce to remove any solids, adjust for seasoning and keep hot until ready to serve.

2. For the sandwich, toast the bread and butter it lightly. Grill and slice the sirloin, chorizo, chipotalas and liguiças. Fill the sandwich with the cooked meats and top with another slice of bread. Lie the cheese slices over the top slice of bread so they overhang the sides of the sandwich. Grill the sandwich so the melted cheese wraps the bread.

3. To serve, top the sandwich with a fried egg and place it in a deep dish. Pour over the hot Francesinha sauce or serve in a small jug to add as desired, with french fries and a cold beer alongside. Serves 4 sandwiches
