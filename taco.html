Taco

A taco is a traditional Mexican dish consisting of a small hand-sized corn or wheat tortilla topped with a filling. The tortilla is then folded around the filling and eaten by hand. A taco can be made with a variety of fillings, including beef, pork, chicken, seafood, vegetables, and cheese, allowing great versatility and variety.
The taco predates the arrival of the Spanish in Mexico. There is anthropological evidence that the indigenous people living in the lake region of the Valley of Mexico traditionally ate tacos filled with small fish.

Ingredients

● 2 teaspoons dried minced onion

● 1 teaspoon salt

● 1 teaspoon chili powder

● 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch

● 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

● 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

● 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper

● 1/4 teaspoon dried minced garlic

● 1/4 teaspoon dried oregan

● 1 pound ground beef

● 1/2 cup water

How to make

1. Mix minced onion, salt, chili powder, cornstarch, cumin, red pepper flakes, cayenne pepper, dried minced garlic, and oregano in a bowl.

2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Crumble ground beef into the hot skillet. Cook and stir until the beef is completely browned, 7 to 10 minutes. Drain and discard any excess grease.

1. Return ground beef to heat. Pour seasoning mixture and water over the beef; stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook until the moisture absorbs into the meat, about 5 minutes.
