Cheese burger

There are many who claim to have created the modern hamburguer, but these stories are still largely contested. It is most likely that it was invented in either the U.S.A. or in the city of Hamburg, in Germany, in the 19th or 20th century. The hamburguer is a sandwich comprisin of a ground beef patty between two toasted buns. Condiments such as pickles, cheese, ketchup, lettuce, etc are often served with the dish.

Ingredients

● 150 grams ground beef

● sliced cheddar cheese

● brioche bun w/ sesame seeds

● san marzano tomato

● pickles

● ketchup

● butter

● lettuce

How to make

1. Spread butter on the sliced buns and lightly toast them.
2. Shape the ground beef into balls, then press them until disc shaped.
3. In a preheated cast iron pan over medium heat, cook the patty for about 6 minutes or until it reaches your prefered doneness. Flip halfway through.
4. Spread ketchup over the bottom bun and place the patty on top of it. Add the cheese, sliced pickles, slices tomatoes and lettuce.
